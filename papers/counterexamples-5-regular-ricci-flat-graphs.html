Counterexamples to a Conjecture on 5-Regular Ricci-Flat Graphs

Abstract

Lei and Bai conjectured that every 5-regular graph which is Ricci-flat with respect to Lin-Lu-Yau curvature is either isomorphic to their 72-vertex graph or is of Cartesian product type. We disprove this dichotomy in the ordinary graph-theoretic Cartesian-product sense. First, we give an explicit 32-vertex counterexample obtained from two cyclic layers over Z/16Z. Second, we construct an infinite family {Hq : q ≥ 5} of 5-regular Ricci-flat graphs which are not ordinary Cartesian products. The graphs Hq are twisted cyclic Petersen bundles, and the curvature verification is reduced to five optimal-assignment matrices, each with a matching cost and a dual lower-bound certificate equal to 6.

Keywords
