Abstract

Network coherence measures the steady-state variance of a networked dynamical system under persistent stochastic disturbance. This paper studies coherence reduction in sparse power-network graphs through grounded-Laplacian leader selection, learning-assisted candidate screening, and bounded edge-weight reallocation. Five MatrixMarket power-network datasets are analyzed, and the results support grounded-Laplacian coherence optimization as an interpretable graph-level robustness tool for sparse infrastructure networks.
