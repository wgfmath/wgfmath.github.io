The Inverse Mostar Index Problem for Bipartite and Chemical Graphs

Abstract

The Mostar index Mo(G) of a connected graph G is a distance-dependent topological descriptor that characterizes molecular graphs via numerical graph invariants. This work addresses two restricted inverse problems for the Mostar index proposed by Alizadeh et al. First, we determine exactly which nonnegative integers occur as Mostar indices of connected bipartite graphs: the answer is N0 \ {1, 3, 5, 13}. Beyond the set-theoretic classification, every admissible value is realized by a connected bipartite molecular graph, that is, by a graph of maximum degree at most four. Second, we answer a question of Alizadeh et al. on chemical graphs by constructing an eleven-vertex chemical graph with Mostar index 5. Consequently, a nonnegative integer serves as the Mostar index of a chemical graph if and only if it is not equal to 1.

Keywords

Mostar index; inverse problem; bipartite graph; chemical graph; molecular graph; chemical tree.
