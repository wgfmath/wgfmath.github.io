Abstract

Zhou, Wang and Bu introduced resistance-regular graphs, namely connected graphs whose resistance distance matrix has constant row sum, and proved several equivalent criteria for this property. This note applies their diagonal criterion to graph joins. We record the join inverse in a shifted-resolvent form, extending the connected-factor formula to arbitrary nonempty, possibly disconnected, factors, and use its diagonal part for resistance-regularity. Consequently, G ∨ H is resistance-regular precisely when two shifted Laplacian resolvent diagonals are constant and satisfy one scalar balance condition. We apply this criterion to prove that Km ∨ H is resistance-regular if and only if H is complete, so the only resistance-regular cones are complete graphs. We also record that complete multipartite graphs are resistance-regular exactly in the balanced case, and give a scalar spectral criterion for joins of walk-regular factors.

Keywords

Resistance distance; resistance-regular graph; graph join; Laplacian matrix; Moore-Penrose inverse; Kirchhoff index.
