Abstract

Let G be a connected simple graph and let RG be its resistance distance matrix. Zhou, Wang and Bu introduced resistance-regular graphs, namely graphs for which all row sums of RG are equal, and gave several equivalent conditions for this property. We present a self-contained spectral form of their characterization: a connected graph is resistance-regular if and only if the diagonal of the Moore-Penrose inverse L+G of its Laplacian is constant. This criterion gives a one-line recognition algorithm, an edge-local Foster-type condition, and, through Kirchhoff's edge formula for uniform spanning trees, a probabilistic interpretation: in a resistance-regular graph every vertex has the same expected degree in a uniform spanning tree. We also record structural consequences and examples.

Keywords

Resistance distance; resistance matrix; Laplacian matrix; Moore-Penrose inverse; Kirchhoff index; spanning trees.
