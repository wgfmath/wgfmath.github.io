Abstract

Let G be a connected graph. The normality of a vertex v is the distance from v to the nearest peripheral vertex, and Norm(G) is the total normality. We determine both the minimum and the maximum of Norm among all trees of order n with exactly k leaves. The minimum has three cases according to the remainder of n - 1 modulo k, and all equality trees are described by level counts from the centre. For the maximum, the boundary cases k = 2, k = n - 2 and k = n - 1 are explicit. In the remaining range 3 ≤ k ≤ n - 3, every maximizer has exactly two peripheral vertices. The maximum is then obtained by taking the largest value, over an explicit finite set of feasible diameters, of a closed formula involving a rooted profile function. The extremal trees are precisely a diametral path with all remaining leaves placed at the centre according to a deepest-level rooted profile.

Keywords
