Abstract

Cizma and Linial introduced graph metrizability through consistent systems of geodesic paths and asked for a classification of the metrizable theta graphs. They proved that Θa,b,c is metrizable when min{a, b, c} = 1 and exhibited Θ3,3,4 as a topologically minimal non-metrizable graph. They left open the cases min{a, b, c} = 2 and (a, b, c) = (3, 3, 3). We resolve this problem. After sorting a ≤ b ≤ c, the theta graph Θa,b,c is metrizable if and only if a ≤ 2 or (a, b, c) = (3, 3, 3). The infinite family Θ2,b,c is handled by a direct potential construction based on Ferrers relations. The exceptional graph Θ3,3,3 is handled by an exact finite certificate.

Keywords

Metrizable graph; consistent path system; shortest paths; theta graph; Ferrers relation; computer-assisted proof.
